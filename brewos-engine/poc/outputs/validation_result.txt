── Note on Discrepancy ─────────────────────────────────────────
  The Moroney (2016) ODE models KINETIC depletion: extraction stops
  when surface coffee is exhausted (psi_s → 0), not when an
  adsorption-desorption equilibrium is reached.

  The Liang (2021) K = 0.717 emerges from an equilibrium between
  adsorption (liquid → solid) and desorption (solid → liquid).
  This re-adsorption term is absent in the Moroney ODE.

  Consequence: Moroney ODE predicts EY → min(E_max, c_sat limited),
  while Liang predicts EY → K × E_max < E_max.

  Resolution: In BrewOS v1, use Liang 2021 as the equilibrium
  anchor and Moroney 2016 for the time-domain kinetics, then
  scale the Moroney output to the Liang equilibrium endpoint.
  Tracked as: BREWOS-TODO-001
